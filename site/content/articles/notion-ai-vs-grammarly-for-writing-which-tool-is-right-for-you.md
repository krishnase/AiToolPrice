# Notion AI vs Grammarly for Writing: Which Tool Is Right for You?

If you write for work, you've probably wondered whether you need a dedicated grammar checker, an AI writing assistant embedded in your workspace, or both. Notion AI and Grammarly are two of the most popular tools in this space — but they serve fundamentally different purposes. This comparison breaks down their features, pricing, and ideal use cases so you can make a confident decision.

## Notion AI: Best for Workspace-Integrated Writing

Notion AI is an add-on to the Notion productivity platform, which means it shines brightest when your writing already lives in Notion. It can draft content, summarize notes, translate text, generate action items, and rewrite passages — all without leaving your document.

**Pricing breakdown:**
- **Free:** Limited AI responses
- **AI add-on:** $8.00/month ($96.00/year) — adds AI to any existing Notion plan
- **Plus:** $10.00/month ($96.00/year, save 20%) — unlimited AI and unlimited pages
- **Business:** $15.00/month ($144.00/year, save 20%) — adds advanced permissions and audit logs

The AI add-on at $8.00/month is genuinely affordable if you're already a Notion user. However, Notion AI is not a grammar tool in the traditional sense. It won't catch a missing comma or flag a passive voice sentence automatically. Instead, it acts more like a capable writing assistant that helps you think, structure, and draft content faster.

**Ideal user:** Project managers, content creators, and teams who already live in Notion and want AI-powered drafting and summarization built into their workflow.

---

## Grammarly: Best for Polished, Error-Free Writing Everywhere

Grammarly is purpose-built for writing quality. Its real-time grammar, spelling, punctuation, and style suggestions follow you across virtually every platform — browsers, Google Docs, Microsoft Word, Slack, and email clients. The depth of its corrections far exceeds what Notion AI offers for proofreading.

**Pricing breakdown:**
- **Free:** Basic corrections
- **Premium:** $30.00/month ($144.00/year, save 60%) — full rewrites, plagiarism check, advanced tone and clarity suggestions
- **Business:** $15.00/month ($144.00/year) per member — adds style guide, analytics, and team management

Grammarly Premium's 60% annual discount makes it $144.00/year — a strong value for professionals who write high-stakes content like reports, proposals, or marketing copy. The plagiarism checker alone can justify the cost for journalists or academics.

**Ideal user:** Marketers, students, professionals, and anyone who writes across multiple platforms and needs consistent, polished output.

---

## Which Should You Choose?

- **Choose Notion AI** if you already use Notion as your primary workspace and want AI assistance for drafting, summarizing, or brainstorming. The $8.00/month add-on is hard to beat for what it offers in context.
- **Choose Grammarly** if writing quality and accuracy are your top priorities, especially if you write across different tools and platforms. The free tier alone beats Notion AI for pure grammar correction.
- **Use both** if you draft in Notion and need professional-grade proofreading before publishing or sending.

## FAQ

**Can Grammarly be used inside Notion?**
Yes. Grammarly's browser extension works within Notion's web app, so you can use both tools simultaneously.

**Is Notion AI worth it if I don't use Notion?**
No. Notion AI only works within the Notion platform. If you don't use Notion, Grammarly or another standalone AI writing tool is a better fit.

**Which tool is cheaper overall?**
Notion AI is significantly cheaper, starting at $8.00/month versus Grammarly Premium at $30.00/month ($144.00/year billed annually).

---

## Conclusion

Notion AI and Grammarly aren't really competitors — they're complementary tools solving different problems. Grammarly wins for grammar, accuracy, and cross-platform polish. Notion AI wins for workflow-integrated drafting and content generation. The best choice depends entirely on where and how you write.
