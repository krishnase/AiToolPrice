# Midjourney Pricing Plans Explained 2026: Which Tier Is Right for You?

Midjourney remains one of the most powerful AI image generation tools available, but choosing the right subscription tier can feel overwhelming — especially as your creative needs evolve. Whether you're a casual hobbyist experimenting with AI art or a professional studio producing hundreds of assets per week, there's a plan designed for you.

In this guide, we break down every Midjourney pricing plan for 2026, evaluating each tier on **value for money, GPU hour allocation, key features, and ideal use cases**. All pricing figures are exact and sourced directly from Midjourney's official plans.

---

## Midjourney Basic Plan — Best for Casual Creators

### Overview
The Basic plan is Midjourney's entry-level offering, giving newcomers and hobbyists a taste of the platform without a significant financial commitment. At just $10.00/month (or $96.00/year with a 20% annual saving), it's the most accessible way to explore AI image generation.

### Pricing
- **Monthly:** $10.00/month
- **Annual:** $96.00/year *(save 20%)*

### Features
- 200 images per month
- 3.3 GPU hours

### Pros
- Very affordable entry point
- Sufficient for light, personal use
- Annual plan delivers solid savings

### Cons
- Limited GPU hours restrict heavy usage
- No Relax mode — all jobs consume Fast GPU time
- 200-image cap can be reached quickly if you iterate frequently

### Best For
Hobbyists, students, and curious creatives who want to experiment with AI art without committing to a larger budget.

## Midjourney Standard Plan — Best for Regular Users

### Overview
The Standard plan is where most individual creators and small-scale freelancers will find their sweet spot. At $30.00/month (or $288.00/year), it dramatically expands your GPU hours and unlocks **Relax mode** — a game-changer for users who want unlimited generation without burning through Fast GPU time.

### Pricing
- **Monthly:** $30.00/month
- **Annual:** $288.00/year *(save 20%)*

### Features
- 15 GPU hours/month (Fast mode)
- Relax mode included (slower but unmetered generation)

### Pros
- Relax mode effectively removes image caps for patient users
- Significant GPU hour jump from Basic
- Strong value for the price point

### Cons
- Relax mode generates images more slowly
- No Stealth mode (your images are publicly visible in the gallery)
- May not be enough Fast GPU time for commercial deadlines

### Best For
Freelance designers, content creators, and power users who need consistent output but don't require privacy or lightning-fast turnaround on every job.

## Midjourney Pro Plan — Best for Professionals

### Overview
Stepping up to $60.00/month ($576.00/year), the Pro plan doubles the Fast GPU hours and introduces **Stealth mode**, which keeps your generated images private and hidden from Midjourney's public gallery. This is essential for commercial and client work.

### Pricing
- **Monthly:** $60.00/month
- **Annual:** $576.00/year *(save 20%)*

### Features
- 30 GPU hours/month (Fast mode)
- Relax mode included
- **Stealth mode** — private image generation

### Pros
- Stealth mode protects client confidentiality
- Generous Fast GPU allocation for professional workflows
- Combines speed with privacy effectively

### Cons
- Price jump is significant from Standard
- Stealth mode is the primary differentiator — you're paying a premium for privacy

### Best For
Agencies, commercial artists, and businesses who need to keep creative work confidential before launch or publication.

## Midjourney Mega Plan — Best for Studios & Power Users

### Overview
The Mega plan is Midjourney's top-tier offering at $120.00/month ($1,152.00/year). With 60 GPU hours of Fast generation and full Stealth mode, this is built for studios, large teams, and any professional producing AI assets at serious volume.

### Pricing
- **Monthly:** $120.00/month
- **Annual:** $1,152.00/year *(save 20%)*

### Features
- 60 GPU hours/month (Fast mode)
- Relax mode included
- Stealth mode included

### Pros
- Maximum Fast GPU time available on any single plan
- Ideal for high-volume production pipelines
- Privacy protected at scale

### Cons
- Expensive — not justified unless you consistently exhaust lower tiers
- No collaborative or team-management features (consider multiple accounts if needed)

### Best For
Creative studios, AI content agencies, and prolific professionals who need maximum generation capacity with full privacy.

## How to Choose the Right Midjourney Plan

**Start with Basic if:** You're brand new to AI image generation, creating purely for personal enjoyment, or simply testing whether Midjourney fits your workflow. The 200-image monthly cap and 3.3 GPU hours are enough to learn the platform without overspending.

**Upgrade to Standard if:** You generate images regularly and need the flexibility of Relax mode. The unmetered (though slower) generation makes it ideal for iterative creative work where speed isn't always critical.

**Choose Pro if:** Privacy is non-negotiable. If you're working on client projects, brand campaigns, or unreleased products, Stealth mode ensures your work stays off Midjourney's public gallery. The doubled GPU hours also support a professional workload.

**Go Mega if:** You're running a studio or agency at scale. If you consistently exhaust your Fast GPU hours on lower plans, the Mega tier's 60-hour allocation gives you the headroom to maintain production momentum.

**Don't forget annual billing** — every plan offers a clean 20% discount when paid yearly, which translates to meaningful savings. For example, Standard drops from $360/year to $288.00/year — saving you $72 annually.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What happens when I run out of Fast GPU hours?**
On Standard, Pro, and Mega plans, you can continue generating images using Relax mode, which is slower but doesn't consume your Fast GPU allowance. On the Basic plan, generation stops until your hours reset monthly.

**Q: Can I switch plans mid-month?**
Yes, Midjourney allows you to upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately, while downgrades typically apply at the start of your next billing cycle.

**Q: Is Stealth mode worth the jump from Standard to Pro?**
If you're doing commercial work, absolutely. Without Stealth mode, every image you generate appears in Midjourney's public gallery, which can expose unreleased creative work to competitors or the public. For personal use, it's less critical — Standard's Relax mode offers excellent value without the privacy premium.
