## Grammarly: Full Pricing & Plan Review

### Overview

Grammarly remains one of the most recognized AI writing assistants on the market. Its browser extension, desktop app, and deep integrations with tools like Google Docs, Microsoft Word, and Slack make it a genuinely practical choice for business teams. But with three distinct tiers now available, choosing the right plan requires a closer look at what you're actually getting — and what you're paying for.

---

### Pricing Breakdown

Grammarly currently offers three plans:

| Plan | Monthly Rate | Annual Total | Annual Savings |
|------|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | — |
| Business | $15.00/member/month | $180.00/year | 0% |
| Premium | $30.00/month | $144.00/year | 60% |

A few things stand out immediately. The **Premium plan** offers a striking 60% discount when billed annually, bringing the total annual cost down to just $144.00 — significantly less than the Business plan's $180.00 per member per year. That pricing relationship is worth unpacking carefully before you commit.

---

### Plan-by-Plan Breakdown

**Free Plan**
Grammarly's free tier covers the basics: spelling, grammar, and punctuation corrections. It's functional for casual use but lacks the depth that professional or business writing demands. There's no style guide enforcement, no analytics, and no team collaboration features. It works as a trial or a supplement, not a standalone solution for teams.

**Business Plan — $15.00/member/month ($180.00/year)**
The Business plan is designed for teams that need centralized control. Key features include a **custom style guide** (ensuring your brand voice stays consistent across every writer), **usage analytics** to track adoption and improvement across your team, and administrative controls for managing members. At $15.00 per member per month with no annual discount applied, costs can scale quickly for larger organizations. A team of 20 writers, for example, would pay $3,600 per year.

**Premium Plan — $30.00/month ($144.00/year)**
The Premium plan is priced for individual users but packs a powerful punch. It includes **full sentence rewrites**, a **plagiarism checker** against billions of web pages, and advanced tone and clarity suggestions. The 60% annual discount makes it remarkably affordable for solo content creators, editors, or freelancers — coming in cheaper annually than the Business plan despite a higher monthly sticker price.

## How to Choose the Right Grammarly Plan

**Choose Free** if you need basic proofreading and aren't ready to commit financially. It's best treated as a starting point.

**Choose Premium** if you're an individual writer, editor, or freelancer who needs full rewrite capabilities and plagiarism detection. The 60% annual discount makes the $144.00/year price point hard to beat.

**Choose Business** if you manage a team and need centralized style enforcement, member oversight, and writing analytics. Just factor in the per-member cost carefully — at $180.00 per member annually with no discount, budget planning is essential.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Grammarly Business worth it for small teams?**
It depends on team size and your need for consistency tools. For teams under five people, Premium plans for each individual may actually cost less annually while still delivering advanced features.

**Q: Why does Premium cost less annually than Business?**
Premium's 60% annual discount reduces the total to $144.00/year, while Business is billed at $15.00/member/month with no annual savings — totaling $180.00 per member per year. For a single user, Premium is the better financial choice.

**Q: Does Grammarly Business include plagiarism checking?**
No. Plagiarism detection is currently a **Premium plan feature only** and is not included in the Business tier — an important consideration for content-heavy teams.
